# Retrospective Playbook

## Overview

This playbook provides guidelines for conducting retrospectives in the Cooperation Toolkit. Retrospectives help teams learn, improve, and adapt.

## Retrospective Structure

### What Went Well

- Celebrate successes
- Identify effective practices
- Recognize contributions
- Build on strengths

### What Could Be Improved

- Identify challenges
- Discuss pain points
- Surface process issues
- Address blockers

### Action Items

- Create specific, actionable improvements
- Assign owners
- Set timelines
- Track progress

## Retrospective Types

### Sprint Retrospective

- Frequency: End of each sprint/iteration
- Duration: 1-2 hours
- Focus: Sprint-specific learnings

### Project Retrospective

- Frequency: End of major milestones
- Duration: 2-4 hours
- Focus: Overall project learnings

### Team Retrospective

- Frequency: Quarterly or as needed
- Duration: 2-3 hours
- Focus: Team dynamics and processes

## Best Practices

1. **Create safe space**: Encourage honest feedback
2. **Focus on process**: Not on individuals
3. **Be specific**: Vague feedback isn't actionable
4. **Follow up**: Ensure action items are completed
5. **Document learnings**: Share insights with the team
